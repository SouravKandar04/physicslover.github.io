Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose:

Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose was a fearless freedom fighter and a key figure in India’s struggle for independence from British colonial rule. Born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, he was a brilliant student and later joined the Indian Civil Services. However, his passion for India’s freedom led him to resign from the civil services and actively participate in the fight against British rule.

Azad Hind Fauj (Indian National Army):

Netaji formed the Indian National Army (INA) in 1942 with the aim of liberating India from British rule. The INA, also known as the Azad Hind Fauj, consisted of Indian soldiers who had been prisoners of war in Southeast Asia.Under Netaji’s leadership, the INA fought alongside the Japanese forces against the British in Burma (now Myanmar) during World War II

Escape to Germany and Japan:

To avoid arrest by the British authorities, Netaji escaped from India in 1941 and traveled to Germany and later to Japan.In Germany, he sought support for India’s independence and established contacts with Nazi officials.In Japan, he collaborated with the Japanese government to form the INA.

Mysterious Disappearance:

Netaji’s fate remains a subject of debate and mystery. In 1945, he reportedly died in a plane crash in Taiwan (then Formosa).However, some theories suggest that he survived and lived incognito in various parts of the world.
